ESILV (École Supérieure d’Ingénieurs Léonard de Vinci) is a French engineering school
located in La Défense, Paris, France. It is part of the Pôle Léonard de Vinci, a
private higher education institution that also includes the business school EMLV
and the digital school IIM.

ESILV was founded in 1995 and delivers a state-recognized engineering degree (Diplôme
d’Ingénieur) accredited by the French Commission des Titres d’Ingénieur (CTI).
The school trains engineers with strong skills in digital technologies, data
science, artificial intelligence, finance, cybersecurity, and industrial systems.

The engineering program at ESILV is accessible after high school or through
admission parallel pathways. The curriculum is organized over five years and
includes a preparatory cycle followed by an engineering cycle. Courses combine
scientific foundations, engineering sciences, and applied projects.

ESILV offers several majors, including:
- Data and Artificial Intelligence
- Financial Engineering
- Digital Systems and IoT
- Industry and Robotics
- Cybersecurity and Networks
- Applied Mathematics and Modeling

Teaching at ESILV emphasizes project-based learning, innovation, and professional
experience. Students must complete multiple internships in France or abroad,
including an international experience requirement.

ESILV has strong international partnerships and offers exchange programs with
universities worldwide. Courses are taught in both French and English, allowing
students to follow international tracks.

The school is located in the heart of La Défense, the main business district of
Paris, providing close connections with major companies and industry partners.
ESILV maintains strong links with the professional world through corporate
projects, internships, and guest lectures.

ESILV is not located in Valenciennes and should not be confused with other
institutions that may have similar names. ESILV specifically refers to École
Supérieure d’Ingénieurs Léonard de Vinci in Paris – La Défense.

The mission of ESILV is to train responsible engineers capable of addressing
technological, economic, and societal challenges in a global context.
